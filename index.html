The Across Healthcare team has had experience building many healthcare and research systems around the world, and they knew privacy, security, and regulatory compliance needed to be of top importance; therefore, Matrix is HIPAA, GDPR, and 21 CFR Part 112 compliant. Many other aspects such as internationalization, accessibility, and interoperability are accounted for at the core of the Matrix architecture.

Rare disease patients and caregivers were initially asked the open-ended question of, "What could we build to make your lives better?" This prompted the following features in the real-world patient tools of Matrix:

  • Caregiver management of one or of many rare disease patients
  • Medication adherence, management, and reminders
  • Symptom and Activity trackers
  • Journaling capabilities
  • File Storage: medical documents, MRIs, videos,
    school forms, genetic reports, etc.
  • Simple data correlation of data captured
  • Ability to share any of this information
  • Disease specific resources to review, educate, and share

Typically, a rare disease patient has mutliple clinicians, and Matrix helps bridge connectivity and continuity with all of them by using the following methods and models:

One Time Access Clinician

Through the patient’s Sharing Center, Matrix allows for sharing of a timebound, password-protected link. This link will allow read-only access to any patient-permissioned data. This could be used at an urgent care clinic, emergency department, or some other single-instance visit.

Regular Clinician

Matrix allows for provisioning of a Clinician user who can log into the platform directly. Once activated as a physician, patients can associate themselves with a clinician and share permissioned data. This maybe used by a primary care clinician or other specialist whom a patient frequently sees. These Clinicians can use Matrix to see their entire patient population.

Center of Excellence (COE) Clinician

With maturity of a rare disease, there are typically global centers of excellence with leading Clinicians. At these COE sites, Matrix can be embedded and will appear as a module within the Clinicians EHR/EMR. Similar capabilities to the Regular Clinician model are accessible and have a seamless flow from the EHR through a framework and integration called SMART on FHIR3.

Patient Advocacy Groups can oversee and deploy Matrix within their respective communities in various configurations. Data can be migrated from other platforms and tools to keep continuity for patients and provide analysis within Matrix. Matrix can facilitate all parts of a Foundation’s needs and can also be deployed in concert with existing platforms. These tools can be found within Matrix:

  • User Management: Patients, Caregivers, Physicians, Curators, and Researchers
  • Messaging and Announcements
  • Study Tools: Informed Consent Management, Case Report Form Editor, Study Mangement
  • Tools, Expert access, and workflow to curate an uploaded patient
  • Resource Center: store and disseminate educational or other materials
  • Population Analytics

Researchers can come from both academic and industry backgrounds. If approved, data feeds can be provided in a variety of industry formats and standards. Matrix also makes it easy to collaborate and create new studies or sub-studies among a Matrix rare disease population. Matrix is a validated software tool that can be audited by a sponsor or regulator if necessary.

"The MWS Foundation is grateful to Jason and Across Healthcare for making the Matrix platform available to MWS patients,” said Deborah Curry, President of the Mowat-Wilson Syndrome Foundation. “Having ready access to important health information at the moment it is needed will not only improve quality of life for patients, the ability to easily share this information will also prove invaluable across the MWS community."

Across Healthcare has a clear vision to empower the entire rare disease community through the Matrix platform with a commitment to listen to feedback about features and capabilities. Any size patient community, large or small, can adopt and leverage Matrix.
