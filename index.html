Matrix can manage your health data and/or others that you care for

  • Medication adherence and management with the ability to set up alerts/reminders
  • Track symptoms & activities that matter to you or your community
  • Create narrative journal entries to chronical your care
  • Upload medical documents, MRIs, videos, audio files, school forms, genetic reports, educational documents, etc.
  • Visualize insights and trends derived from your data
  • Securely share any information with loved ones, clinicians, or others
  • Disease specific resources to review, educate, and share
  • Advance research through participation in surveys and patient reported outcomes
  • Multi-language support
  • Available on your computer, tablet, iOS devices, and Android devices

Matrix can allow you to interact closely with your patients

  • Access patient data through several available options:
    1. A link and security code via a patient's sharing center.
    2. A direct login to Matrix where you can see all your patients.
    3. An embedded app within your electronic health record.
    ​
  • Have real-time communication with patients
  • View aggregated population analytics for all your patients
  • Collect data for a trial/study via Matrix
  • Receive educational materials from foundations and researchers to stay up to date on the latest evidence

Typically, a rare disease patient has mutliple clinicians, and Matrix helps bridge connectivity and continuity with all of them by using the following methods and models:

One Time Access Clinician

Through the patient’s Sharing Center, Matrix allows for sharing of a timebound, password-protected link. This link will allow read-only access to any patient-permissioned data. This could be used at an urgent care clinic, emergency department, or some other single-instance visit.

Regular Clinician

Matrix allows for provisioning of a Clinician user who can log into the platform directly. Once activated as a physician, patients can associate themselves with a clinician and share permissioned data. This maybe used by a primary care clinician or other specialist whom a patient frequently sees. These Clinicians can use Matrix to see their entire patient population.

Center of Excellence (COE) Clinician

With maturity of a rare disease, there are typically global centers of excellence with leading Clinicians. At these COE sites, Matrix can be embedded and will appear as a module within the Clinicians EHR/EMR. Similar capabilities to the Regular Clinician model are accessible and have a seamless flow from the EHR through a framework and integration called SMART on FHIR3.
